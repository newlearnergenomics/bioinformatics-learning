### Biology Foundation (6+ hours of videos)

#### DNA Structure (Amoeba Sisters)
- DNA is a double helix made of 4 bases: A, T, G, C
- A always pairs with T. G always pairs with C.
- A gene is a section of DNA that codes for a protein

#### Central Dogma (Khan Academy)
The flow of life:DNA to RNA using Transcription and RNA to protein via Translation
#### DNA Replication (Khan Academy - 2 videos)
- Cell copies DNA before dividing
- DNA polymerase reads DNA and makes an exact copy
- Semi-conservative replication

#### Transcription (Khan Academy - 2 videos + Amoeba Sisters)
- RNA polymerase reads one gene from DNA
- Makes a temporary RNA copy
- RNA leaves nucleus and carries message

#### Translation (Khan Academy - 2 videos + Amoeba Sisters)
- Ribosome reads RNA three letters at a time (codons)
- Each three letters = one amino acid
- Amino acids link together = protein is made

#### RNA-seq (StatQuest)
- Question: Which genes are turned on in a cell?
- Method: Extract RNA, sequence it, count which RNAs are present
- Result: High RNA = gene is active. Low RNA = gene is quiet.
- Purpose: Find which genes are different between healthy and sick cells

## Why This Matters for Bioinformatics

When we sequence DNA or RNA, we're reading these ACGT letters.
- Our job = understand what these sequences mean
- Find patterns in the data
- Compare sequences between healthy and diseased cells
- Use tools to automate the analysis
